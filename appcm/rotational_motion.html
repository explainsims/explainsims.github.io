Tangential Velocity

Every point on a rigid body rotates through the same angle, but points farther from the centre travel a longer arc — so they move faster.

v = r · ω

Tangential Acceleration

When angular acceleration α is non-zero, every point accelerates tangentially. The magnitude scales linearly with radius.

at = r · α

Centripetal Acceleration

Even at constant ω, each point requires centripetal acceleration directed inward to maintain circular motion. It scales with ω².

ac = r · ω²

AP Physics C Note

The total linear acceleration is the vector sum of tangential and centripetal components. They are always perpendicular to each other.

a = √(at² + ac²)
