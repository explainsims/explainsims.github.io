2 Physics Reference

1. Kinematics of Constant Acceleration

When an object slides from rest (v₀ = 0), the distance traveled d is related to acceleration a and time t by:

d = ½ a t² ⇒ a = 2d / t²

2. Newton's Second Law & Friction

Forces acting along the ramp: gravity (pulling down) and kinetic friction (resisting motion). Net force is F_net = m·a.

  • Gravity component along ramp: F_g = mg sin(θ)
  • Normal force: N = mg cos(θ)
  • Kinetic friction: f_k = μ_k N = μ_k mg cos(θ)
mg sin(θ) − μ_k mg cos(θ) = ma

Notice mass (m) appears in every term and cancels — mass does not affect the acceleration of the sliding object.

3. Solving for the Coefficient (μ_k)

Dividing by mass and isolating μ_k:

μ_k = (g·sin(θ) − a) / (g·cos(θ))

Use standard gravity g = 9.81 m/s².

Friction Lab Guide

What is this?

An inclined-plane experiment to determine the coefficient of kinetic friction (μₖ) between two surfaces. Pick a block material and a ramp material, set the angle, then run trials to time the slide and back-calculate μₖ.

Setup

Object Material & Ramp Material — choose the two surfaces in contact. The true μₖ depends on both.

Angle — the incline angle in degrees. Below the friction angle (tan θ < μₛ) the block won't slide.

Distance — the slide distance in metres, used in the kinematics equation to extract acceleration.

Mass — included as a distractor. μₖ from kinematics is mass-independent on a uniform incline; verify this for yourself.

Color — cosmetic, for distinguishing blocks across runs.

Run a trial

Press Run. The block animates down the ramp (or jiggles in place if the angle is below the friction angle), and a row is added to the data table with the time and computed μₖ.

Physics reference

Open the theory panel to review the free-body diagram, Newton's 2nd law along the incline, and the formula μₖ = (g·sin θ − a) / (g·cos θ).

Verify the coefficient

Use the data from a successful slide to calculate μₖ yourself. After three attempts the step-by-step solution is revealed. Repeat trials and compare your value to the true coefficient.
