Friction Lab Guide

What is this?

An inclined-plane experiment to determine the coefficient of kinetic friction (μₖ) between two surfaces. Pick a block material and a ramp material, set the angle, then run trials to time the slide and back-calculate μₖ.

Setup

Object Material & Ramp Material — choose the two surfaces in contact. The true μₖ depends on both.

Angle — the incline angle in degrees. Below the friction angle (μₖ = tan θ) the block won't slide.

Distance — the slide distance in metres. Used in the kinematics equation to extract acceleration.

Mass — included as a distractor. μₖ from kinematics is mass-independent on a uniform incline; verify this for yourself.

Colour — cosmetic, for distinguishing blocks across runs.

Run a trial

Press Run. The block animates down the ramp (or stays put if the angle is below the friction angle), and a row is added to the data table with the time, computed μₖ, and the temperature/pressure distractors.

Physics reference

Use the theory panel to review the free-body diagram, Newton's 2nd law along the incline, and the formula μₖ = tan θ − a/(g cos θ).

Verify the coefficient

Repeat trials with different angles, distances, and masses for the same material pair. Average your computed μₖ values and compare against the true coefficient revealed in the analysis panel.
