1. System Properties

We are using a uniform rod with:

  • Mass M = 9 kg
  • Length L = 10 m

Moment of inertia about the CM:

Icm = (1/12)ML²
= (1/12)(9)(10)²
= 75 kg·m²
2. Parallel-Axis Theorem

When the pivot is at distance d from the CM:

I = Icm + Md²

Move the slider and watch the result update in real time:

I = 75 + 9(0.00)²
I = 75 + 0.0
I = 75.0 kg·m²
3. Key Insight

The inertia follows a parabola — it's minimum at the CM (d = 0) and increases as the pivot moves toward either end. This means it's always hardest to rotate an object around a point far from its centre of mass.
